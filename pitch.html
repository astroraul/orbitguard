02 / THE SOLUTION

One pane of glass.
Operator-grade. Browser-native.

OrbitGuard ingests live TLE catalogs and breakup feeds, propagates them in real time, and surfaces per-asset MMOD threat scores, flux estimates, and conjunction context — without a server, an install, or a license file.

01

Real-time situational awareness

3D LEO globe with SGP4-propagated catalogs from CelesTrak, refreshed continuously. Pan, click any object, see its full orbital state.

02

AI-derived threat scoring

Heuristic v3.1 fuses conjunction proximity, debris density at altitude, recent breakup activity, and asset cross-section into a single 0–100 risk index.

03

Mission-ready reporting

Export per-orbit or per-asset risk briefings as PDF/PNG. Designed to be consumed by both engineers and underwriters.

06 / WHY NOW

Three tailwinds collide.

Mega-constellations

Starlink, Kuiper, Guowang, and IRIS² will push active satellites past 50,000 by 2030. Every new asset is a new conjunction surface and a new customer for risk products.

Regulatory mandates

FCC's 5-year deorbit rule (2024), EU Space Law (in trilogue), and emerging UN STM frameworks are converting "best practice" into legal requirements — with audit trails.

Insurance hardening

Space insurance premiums hit record highs in 2024 after Intelsat 33e. Underwriters now demand third-party MMOD risk attestations on policies above $50M.

Browser-native compute is finally enough

WebGL2 + WebGPU let us propagate >30k objects client-side at 60fps. The "install our desktop client" era is ending.
